Problem Statement & Abstract

The rapid digitalization of education has significantly increased the reliance on certificates as proof of knowledge. However, traditional certificate management systems rely on physical documents or centralized digital storage, which are prone to forgery, duplication, loss, and unauthorized modification.

To address these issues, Credlytic introduces a decentralized certificate management system. The system utilizes Non-Fungible Tokens (NFTs) to convert certificates into unique digital assets that are securely recorded on the Aptos blockchain, ensuring they cannot be altered or misused.

Core Objectives

  • Develop a secure, decentralized platform for issuing and managing academic certificates.
  • Implement Non-Fungible Tokens (NFTs) to represent credentials as unique, tamper-proof digital assets.
  • Ensure data immutability by storing records on the Aptos blockchain network.
  • Enable instant, reliable verification of certificates without manual administrative processes.

Methodology & Technology Stack

The methodology combines certificate generation, NFT-based representation, blockchain storage, wallet integration, and instant verification into a unified workflow. Administrators input student data via a secure frontend, triggering a Python (Flask) backend to programmatically generate the digital document. This asset is uploaded to Supabase, while an immutable transaction hash is minted to the Aptos Testnet ledger.
